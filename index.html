The Feedback Loop: Re-defining "Sales Ready"

Context (Hyper Island): Marketing ran broad Facebook/LinkedIn ads and passed single-click leads directly to us as SQLs. After calling them, we realized many had never even heard of the brand. They were completely unqualified.
Solution: Instead of letting bad leads ruin conversion data, I instituted a strict feedback loop. I manually downgraded their Lifecycle Stage back to MQL, tagged the specific "Disqualified Reason", and routed them back to Marketing for top-of-funnel nurturing.

🔄 The Downgrade Routing Logic
How I aligned Sales and Marketing by fixing the data flow:
1. Identify Poor SQL Quality
Consolidated feedback from 3+ sales reps: "These leads are too cold. High bounce rate on calls."
2. HubSpot Property Update
Changed Lifecycle Stage from SQL ➔ MQL.
3. Add 'Disqualified Reason' Tag
Tagged with: "Not Nurtured Enough". Passed back to Marketing to trigger the educational email sequence.

Example: Course starts in 14 days. Lead has shown interest but hasn't booked a meeting.

IF/THEN Logic Branch
Condition: Did they open the syllabus link? If Yes -> Schedule Sales Call task. If No -> Proceed to Day 4 email.
Day 7: Drive Urgency Trigger: No action
"Only 7 days left until the cohort begins." - Shift tone to scarcity and high urgency.
